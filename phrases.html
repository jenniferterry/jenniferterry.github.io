Key Phrases

Three phrases to help you get by in Paris

When visiting a new city it is always a good idea to have a list of key phrases to help you find your way around.

We have come up with 3 of the most important phrases that we use on a daily basis when we are in Paris.

  • 1. Combien ? : How much?
      • This is an easy yet really useful word for when shopping or on public transport.

    Simply asking 'Combien?' and pointing at the item you want, will allow you to buy food, clothes and tickets for museums, trains or theatre performances

  • 2. Où sont les toilettes ? : Where are the toilets?
      • If you are in a café, and it is in a touristy area, then café may not allow you to use the toilet unless you are a patron.

    So, we suggest that in this situation you ask for a coffee (un café s’il vous plait) and then ask to use the toilet (où sont les toilettes?)

  • 3. Où est la station de métro la plus proche? : Where is the closest metro station?
      • Paris is a big place, and the easiest way to get around is by using the metro, which is similar to London’s underground, or New York’s subway.

    Although there are a lot of metro stations in Paris, they are not always easy to spot which is why this phrase is really helpful!
