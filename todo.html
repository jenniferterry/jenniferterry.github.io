What to do in Paris

Top 5 things to do in Paris

Paris is a city jam-packed with opportunities for taking in the views, learning about the past and looking to the future.

What is even better, that no matter what your budget, there are loads of things to do. Here are 5 of the top things that we think cannot be missed.

If you have other ideas for things to do in paris, then do let us know by getting in touch via social media or email- check out the links at the bottom of the page!

1. A cruise on the Seine

If relaxing and taking in the views is your thing then don't miss out on a romantic cruise down one of the most picturesque rivers in Europe.

2. Visit the Eiffel Tower

    No trip to Paris is complete without a trip to the Eiffel tower

Although the base of the tower has been secured, the views from the top are no less breathtaking!

We recommend booking before you get to the tower to avoid the queues. This is easy and quick to do online.

If you are in Paris for a romantic trip away, then you should consider getting a meal in the resturant at the top of the tower

There are not many more romantic things to do then watch the sun go down over Paris!

3. A trip to the Louvre museum

For those of you who love architecture, history and amazing art, the Louvre museum is an absolute must on any trip to Paris.

Even though we have been to Paris numerous times, we pay a vist to the Louvre every single trip. Although the queues can be long, they are worth it.

Also, remember that if you are an EU citizen and under 25, if you take your passport to the museum, you can get it free!

4. Shopping in 'Les Galeries Lafayette'

Within minutes of the Opéra Garnier is one of the world’s most famous place to go shopping!

There are thousands of international brands that range from affordable to luxury.

Whether you are into fashion, jewelery, beauty, gastronomy or culture, you will have a great time here!

5. Breakfast from a Parisian patisserie

    Croissants, pain au chocolat, or brioche with chocolat chaud and fruit - you cannot get more French then that!

Down almost every street in Paris you will find a patisserie in Paris. The smell of freshly baked bread and pastries is almost intoxiating!

It would be a crime to not try out some of the baked goods. You can either get a selection to take away, or find a patisserie that has a cafe attached and enjoy your meal inside

Many patisseries now even make allergen free options, so even if you are gluten or lactose intolerant, you can still enjoy a traditional Parisian treat!
